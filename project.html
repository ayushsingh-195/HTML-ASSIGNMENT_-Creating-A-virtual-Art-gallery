  1. Bharat Mata (Mother India)

    Painted by Abanindranath Tagore in 1905. This work is a cornerstone of the Bengal School of Art.
    It depicts a saffron-clad woman, portrayed as a goddess, holding symbolic objects in her four hands.

  2. Bani Thani

    This iconic painting is from the Kishangarh school of painting, created by the artist Nihal Chand in 1750.
    It is often compared to the Mona Lisa and is celebrated for its elegant and idealized features.

  3. Shakuntala

    A masterpiece by Raja Ravi Varma from 1870. Varma is renowned for blending European academic art techniques with purely Indian themes.

  4. Three Girls (Group of Three Girls)

    Painted by Amrita Sher-Gil in 1935, this is a landmark work in Indian modern art.

  5. Horses

    The horse was a recurring and powerful motif in the works of M. F. Husain, a leading figure of the Progressive Artists' Group.
